The Problem HAKI Solves

Modern CPU transistors are smaller than a strand of DNA. At this scale, quantum effects, electromagnetic interference, cosmic rays, and deliberate electrical attacks can cause a CPU to silently compute the wrong answer — with no error flag, no crash, no warning.

This is called Silent Data Corruption (SDC). Meta's 2021 research found it at a rate of 1 event per several thousand servers per day. At hyperscale, this means thousands of invisible errors daily. Unity Technologies lost $110 million in a single quarter from corrupted data flowing undetected through their systems.

HAKI is the first software-deployable system that detects these failures in real-time, during the computation itself.

The Digital Strain Gauge

Civil engineers attach strain gauges to bridges — sensors that detect microscopic physical deformation before a crack becomes a collapse. HAKI applies the same principle to silicon.

Every 10 milliseconds, HAKI fires Ghost Instructions through the CPU: a precisely crafted sequence of arithmetic operations whose physical timing signature is uniquely determined by the electrical and thermal state of the hardware. If the hardware is lying — whether from attack, wear, or a cosmic-ray hit — the timing changes. HAKI measures that change to sub-nanosecond precision.
